## Notes

The "Simmer" Time: Videos don't rank for high-value buying keywords overnight. An average age of nearly a year shows that search algorithms need time to test a video. They want to see consistent user engagement, watch time, and click-through rates before they trust a video enough to show it to someone ready to make a purchase.

The Sweet Spot of Freshness: While it takes time to rank, the data also proves that ancient videos don't win. For transactional searches (like "best marketing software 2026" or product comparisons), buyers want current information. If a video is much older than a year, it likely starts getting replaced by newer, updated content.

The Long-Game Strategy: If you are a creator or a brand, you can't expect instant sales from a brand-new YouTube upload. You have to plant the seeds early, knowing the video won't peak in search for about 9 months. It also means you should plan to refresh or re-record your most important product videos every year to defend your rankings.
